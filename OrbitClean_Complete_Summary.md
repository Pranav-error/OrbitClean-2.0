## 7. BOOTCAMP — REMOTE SENSING FUNDAMENTALS *(Dr. P.G. Devakar)*

### Satellite Basics
- Earth observation satellites orbit at **500–800 km** altitude
- Use **sun-synchronous positioning** for consistent lighting conditions
- Pointing accuracy, drift rate, and jitter all affect image quality

### Electromagnetic Spectrum & Spectral Signatures
- Different surfaces reflect differently across wavelengths — this is their unique "fingerprint"
- Key bands used in waste/land analysis:

| Band | Wavelength | What it detects |
|---|---|---|
| Green | 0.5–0.6 µm | Vegetation health |
| Red | 0.6–0.7 µm | Bare soil, stress |
| Near IR (NIR) | 0.7–0.9 µm | Dense vegetation, water bodies |
| Shortwave IR (SWIR) | 1.5–2.5 µm | Moisture, minerals, disturbed land |

- **Water** = very low reflectance (absorbs most light)
- **Sandy/bare soil** = high reflectance
- **Vegetation** = characteristic NIR peak
- **Mixed waste/dump sites** = anomalous spectral signature combining disturbed soil + plastic + stressed vegetation

### Spatial Resolution
- **1m** — individual objects visible (high-resolution commercial)
- **5.8m** — building-level features
- **10m (Sentinel-2)** — ward-level dump site detection *(our use case)*
- **24–30m (Landsat)** — broad land use classification

### Supervised Classification
- Training set → model → test set validation
- Maximum Likelihood technique used operationally in India (Karnataka crop mapping example shown)
- Directly applicable to waste site classification from satellite imagery

### Bengaluru Urbanisation Example
- Satellite imagery from 1996, 2002, 2013 showed rapid urban sprawl
- Demonstrates feasibility of satellite-based land use change detection in Bengaluru specifically

## 8. BOOTCAMP — GIS & QGIS *(Dr. K.V. Sudeesh)*

### GIS Fundamentals
- **Definition:** Computer-based system to store, retrieve, analyse, model, and display spatial data
- **Core principle:** *"Layer concept is the single most important idea in GIS"*
- Transforms raw data → information → knowledge

### Five Components of GIS
1. **Methods** — analytical procedures
2. **Hardware** — computers and devices
3. **Software** — QGIS (open-source tool taught)
4. **Data** — spatial and non-spatial
5. **People** — the human layer

### GIS Data Types

**Vector Data** (precise, feature-based, smaller file size):
- **Points** — single location (X,Y coordinate) — e.g., a dumping site, a building
- **Lines** — connected points — e.g., roads, collection routes, rivers
- **Polygons** — closed boundaries — e.g., city wards, lakes, land parcels

**Raster Data** (grid of pixels, good for continuous surfaces):
- Satellite Imagery — LISS3, Sentinel-2, Landsat (GeoTIFF format)
- Digital Elevation Models — SRTM, ASTER, LiDAR
- Thematic/Classified Maps — NDVI, land use/cover, soil type

### GIS Data Formats
| Format | Type | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `.shp` (Shapefile) | Vector | Points, lines, polygons with attributes |
| `.geojson` | Vector | Web-friendly, human-readable |
| `.tif` (GeoTIFF) | Raster | Standard satellite imagery format |
| `.img` | Raster | Remote sensing software format |

### GPS & Trilateration
- GPS = network of 24+ satellites providing lat/long coordinates
- Works via trilateration — distance from 3+ satellites pinpoints location
- Applications relevant to our solution: vehicle tracking, distance to dumpyard, route distance

### Key QGIS Plugins (Taught in Bootcamp)

**1. QuickMapServices**
- Adds Google Satellite, OpenStreetMap, Bing, ESRI basemaps into QGIS
- Use: Visualise ward layout with real-world imagery as background

**2. QuickOSM**
- Downloads roads, buildings, infrastructure from OpenStreetMap
- Use: Get road network of target ward for routing analysis

**3. ORS Tools**
- Routing and network analysis within QGIS
- Features: shortest path, route optimisation, travel time/distance, service area analysis
- Use: Optimise waste collection vehicle routes across the ward

## 9. SWM RULES 2026 — POLICY CONTEXT

### What's New
- **Government of India** notified new Solid Waste Management Rules, 2026
- Replaces SWM Rules 2016
- **Effective: 1 April 2026** *(11 days from today)*
- Issued under Environment (Protection) Act, 1986

### Scope
Applies to all urban and rural local bodies including households, commercial establishments, hotels, hospitals, schools, markets, railway stations, and airports.
*(Excludes industrial, hazardous, biomedical, and e-waste — covered under separate rules)*

### Major Change — Mandatory 4-Stream Segregation

| Stream | Colour | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| **Wet Waste** | Green | Kitchen waste, food scraps, vegetable & fruit waste |
| **Dry Waste** | Blue | Paper, plastic, glass, metal |
| **Sanitary Waste** | Red | Diapers, sanitary pads, tissues, paper |
| **Special Care / Hazardous Household** | Black | Paint, batteries, CFL bulbs, e-waste, medicines |

> Segregation **must be done at source** (home or business location)

### Duties of Waste Generators
- Segregate into 4 streams
- Hand over only to authorised collectors
- Not burn or dump in drains/water bodies
- Pay user charges to local bodies

### Bulk Waste Generators (EBWGR)
Buildings qualifying as bulk generators (>20,000 sqm OR >100 kg waste/day):
- Must process wet waste on-site
- Must obtain EBWGR Certificate
- Subject to Polluter Pays Penalties
- Examples: Apartments (~800 flats), hotels, hospitals, malls, government offices

### Digital SWM Portal
- Mandatory digital tracking and online auditing of waste flows
- OrbitClean's dashboard directly fulfils this requirement at ward level

### Relevance to OrbitClean
| SWM 2026 Requirement | OrbitClean Feature |
|---|---|
| 4-stream segregation enforcement | AI waste classification (Organic/Recyclable/Hazardous/Residual) |
| Digital SWM Portal / audit trail | Leaflet.js dashboard with geotagged collection records |
| Polluter Pays Penalties | Satellite evidence of illegal dumping for enforcement |
| Stream-specific vehicle assignment | Smart vehicle dispatch based on waste type |
| Bulk generator compliance monitoring | Satellite + footprint analysis to flag non-compliant sites |

## 11. OUR SOLUTION — ORBITCLEAN

**Full Name:** OrbitClean — Space-Enabled Waste Intelligence for Circular Economy and Illegal Waste Dumping

### Problem Statement
Bengaluru generates over 5,000 tonnes of municipal solid waste daily. Illegal dumping persists across peri-urban areas, lakebeds, and vacant layouts. Current systems rely on citizen complaints, manual inspections, and fixed-route waste collection — leading to delayed response, reactive management, and inefficient mixed-waste transportation. Mixed waste transport increases fuel wastage, carbon emissions, and reduces material recovery value.

### Solution Architecture

```
Sentinel-2 Satellite Imagery
        ↓
Google Earth Engine Processing
        ↓
QGIS Spatial Analysis (Ward Boundaries + Road Network)
        ↓
Hotspot Detection (Anomalous spectral signatures)
        ↓
YOLOv8 + OpenCV — Waste Classification
(Organic / Recyclable / Hazardous / Residual)
        ↓
K-Means Clustering — Group hotspots by type & density
        ↓
Genetic Algorithm — Smart Vehicle Route Optimisation
        ↓
Flask/FastAPI Backend
        ↓
Leaflet.js Dashboard
(Hotspots · Waste Types · Optimised Routes · Collection Records)
```

### Key Features
1. **Satellite Detection** — Identifies illegal dumping hotspots using open-source Sentinel-2 imagery
2. **AI Waste Classification** — YOLOv8 segregates waste into Organic, Recyclable, Hazardous, Residual
3. **Volume Estimation** — Predicts waste scale and composition at each site
4. **Smart Vehicle Assignment** — Routes dispatch correct collection vehicle type by waste category
5. **Digital Traceability** — Detection → Classification → Collection → Recovery chain

### Technology Stack

**Space & Geospatial:**
- Sentinel-2 (Copernicus Open Access Hub)
- ISRO Bhuvan portal (Indian geospatial layers)
- Google Earth Engine (satellite data processing)
- QGIS & GeoPandas (spatial analysis)
- Ward boundary + road network overlay

**AI & Routing:**
- Python (core language)
- YOLOv8 & OpenCV (waste image classification)
- Scikit-learn (ML modelling)
- Genetic Algorithm (route optimisation)
- Flask / FastAPI (backend)
- Leaflet.js (dashboard / map visualisation)

### Implementation Plan

| Phase | Days | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Data & Detection** | 1–5 | Collect waste sample images; download Sentinel-2 data for Thanisandra ward; identify hotspots; prepare labelled dataset |
| **Phase 2: AI Classification & Clustering** | 6–9 | Fine-tune YOLOv8; classify waste into 4 streams; apply K-Means clustering by type and density |
| **Phase 3: Routing & Dashboard** | 10–14 | Implement route optimisation; build Flask backend; develop Leaflet.js dashboard |

### Expected Outcomes
- Early detection of illegal dumping → reduced landfill dependency
- Improved material recovery rates
- Lower greenhouse gas emissions through optimised routing
- End-to-end digital accountability from detection to recovery
- Compliance infrastructure for SWM Rules 2026

## 14. SUGGESTED NARRATIVE & PITCH

### One-Line Pitch
> *"OrbitClean — built on ground truth from Thanisandra ward, aligned with India's SWM Rules 2026, using satellite intelligence to detect, classify, and route — turning India's new waste law into a system that actually works on the ground."*

### Full Narrative for Presentation
> *"Bengaluru generates 5,000 tonnes of waste every day — yet as we saw firsthand during our field survey in Thanisandra on 7 March, waste is still piling up at roadsides and vacant plots, unsegregated, unmonitored, and uncollected. The bootcamp taught us that every surface has a spectral signature. Illegal dump sites create a unique mixed signature — disturbed soil, reflective plastic, stressed vegetation — that Sentinel-2 can detect from 500 km above. We take that signal, overlay it on ward boundaries as polygons, mark dump sites as point hotspots, pull the road network as lines using QuickOSM, and use ORS Tools or our custom routing engine to dispatch the right vehicle — because with SWM Rules 2026 taking effect on 1 April, each waste stream now requires a different vehicle. The result is OrbitClean: a complete, law-aligned, satellite-powered waste intelligence system built entirely on the tools and frameworks taught in this bootcamp."*

### Anticipated Judge Questions & Answers

**Q: How do you get training data for YOLOv8 in 24 hours?**
A: We use the open-source TACO (Trash Annotations in Context) dataset for base training, supplemented by field photos from our 7 March survey in Thanisandra. We fine-tune rather than train from scratch.

**Q: How do you estimate waste volume from satellite imagery?**
A: YOLOv8 bounding boxes give pixel area. At Sentinel-2's 10m resolution, each pixel = 100 sqm. Combined with spectral intensity as a density proxy, we estimate relative volume per site.

**Q: Why Sentinel-2 specifically?**
A: Free and open-source via Copernicus; 10m spatial resolution in key bands; 5-day revisit cycle for temporal monitoring; well-supported by Google Earth Engine and QGIS.

**Q: Is 10m resolution sufficient for detecting dump sites?**
A: A dump site large enough to require a dedicated collection vehicle is typically >100 sqm — at least one full Sentinel-2 pixel. Our ground truth from Thanisandra (Site 1 and Site 2) shows sites that are clearly detectable at this resolution.

**Q: How does this align with the new SWM 2026 rules?**
A: The rules mandate 4-stream segregation, a digital SWM portal, and Polluter Pays penalties — OrbitClean provides exactly these: AI-based 4-stream classification, a geotagged digital audit trail, and satellite evidence for enforcement.
